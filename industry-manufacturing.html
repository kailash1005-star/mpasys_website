You are not short of ambition. You are short of a partner who respects how a factory actually runs.

Manufacturers do not need another dashboard. They need fewer manual reports, faster supplier handoffs, and tools that production managers will open on a Tuesday afternoon when the line is under pressure.

The pressure points

Three places where manufacturing quietly loses time and margin.

Every operation we walk into has some version of these. They are not production problems — they are data and workflow problems, and they are fixable.

Reporting by hand

Production, quality and shift reports assembled manually from spreadsheets and machine exports — hours of skilled time spent copying numbers instead of acting on them.

Fragmented supplier workflows

Orders, confirmations and delays tracked across email, phone and disconnected systems, so problems surface late and the same questions get asked twice.

Knowledge that walks out the door

The experienced operator who knows why the line behaves the way it does retires — and that judgement is not written down anywhere a system can use.

How we turn it around

Purpose-built AI and Zoho, designed for the floor — not the boardroom slide.

01

Automated operational reporting

We connect your machine data, ERP and spreadsheets into reports that build themselves — shift summaries, quality trends, OEE — so managers spend the morning deciding, not collating.

02

Supplier & order workflow automation

Zoho-based workflows that track orders, confirmations and exceptions in one place, with automatic alerts when something slips — fewer surprises, fewer status-chasing calls.

03

Internal AI knowledge tools

A GDPR-compliant assistant trained on your manuals, SOPs and historical fixes, so a newer operator can ask "why is this happening" and get your best people's answer.

04

Intelligent automation of repeat decisions

Approvals, classifications and routing automated with rules and AI that stay auditable — you keep control, the routine work stops eating skilled hours.
