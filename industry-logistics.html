Your industry

When every exception is handled by a phone call, the system is the bottleneck.

Logistics runs on tight margins and tighter timelines. The cost is rarely a single failure — it is the slow drag of manual coordination: chasing a status, re-keying an order, telling a customer something you already told them yesterday.

We connect the moving parts so information flows on its own, exceptions surface early, and your team spends its time on the shipments that actually need a human.

The pressure points

Three places where supply chains quietly leak time and trust.

They look like operational problems. They are workflow problems — and that means they can be designed out.

Manual dispatch decisions

Routing and prioritisation made from memory and spreadsheets, so the right call depends on who happens to be on shift rather than the data in front of them.

Exceptions found too late

Delays and SLA risks surface when the customer calls, not when the data first shows them — turning a manageable issue into a fire drill.

Fragmented customer communication

Updates scattered across email, phone and portals mean customers chase you for status, and your team answers the same question five times a day.

How we turn it around

Connected workflows and AI that fix the root, not the symptom.

01

Unified order & shipment tracking

Zoho-based workflows that pull orders, statuses and exceptions into one live view — so dispatch and customer service work from the same picture instead of separate inboxes.

02

Early-warning exception alerts

Rules and AI that flag at-risk SLAs the moment the data shows them, routing the right exception to the right person before it becomes a customer complaint.

03

Automated customer updates

Proactive, consistent status communication triggered by real events — fewer chasing calls, less re-keying, and a customer experience that feels in control.

04

Decision-support AI for dispatch

An assistant that surfaces the relevant constraints and history so routing and prioritisation decisions are consistent — not dependent on who is on shift.
